CrowdSurge

Redesign of the CrowdSurge ticketing platform helping people buy tickets directly from artists, more quickly and easily.

To meet the growing business demands and the changing nature of how people buy tickets, I lead the redesign of CrowdSurge's global online ticketing platform to create a responsive, mobile first e-commerce platform.

Problem: Miniature Desktop Design

Similar to our competitors (screengrabs below) our interface was limited by UI components designed for desktop, like drop down select buttons. The pre-existing scenarios limited the user in two major ways; One way was that the ability to display data was stuck in these drop down selects - with drop down selects, you are limited to only showing one data point (possibly two if you hack it in.) Which makes it hard to compare options upfront. This results in the user having to expand and close the option select drop-down multiple times in order to see their possible options and conduct multiple searches.

Design Process: Ask old ladies to buy Daft Punk tickets

To get real feedback on our initial wireframes we prototyped and tested the designs. Nothing gives clarity to a design like a 55 year old woman trying to select Daft Punk tickets using your prototype. Being able to design, build and test these concepts in under a few days was critical to testing our assumptions and getting real feedback early on.


This video shows Loraine from Missouri, a 55 year old woman that found this interface a joy to use.

Learn more about how we operate at CrowdSurge and the agile design process I implemented here.

Solution: Enhanced One-Touch Ticket Select Interface

By breaking out those options and the data points from option select dropdowns, we visualized available ticket options and displayed additional secondary information. This made it possible for a simple, one touch ticket select process versus having to open up an option select drop-down multiple times.

Authentic Brand Integraitons

As a white label product that is customized by our clients (ranging from Paul McCartney to Kendrick Lamar) it was important to make sure that the visual design followed the hierrarchy of the most important design elements.


By testing custom implementations of the platform I created a set of rules and best practices that allowed our visual designers to easily balance the clients brand design and the design of the application.
