Sortey

Visualize your browsing history.

This is a side project designed to visualize your browsing history. Sortey takes the things you have visited online and automatically organizes it in a visual way that helps you find things again.

Role: Design Lead

Goal: Find product market fit.

Timeframe: Launch 2018

The Challenge

We look at a staggering amount of products across multiple devices, but have very poor recall.

67 (average number of products a person views in a week)

300 (average number of products a person views in a month)

8% of our time online is spent shopping.

Finding Product Market Fit

While the proof of concept is still in development, finding product market fit and validating a demand beforehand is critical to guiding the product development.

Constructing a Brand Identity

While finding product market fit, I have begun early brand exploration around the look and feel of Sortey. With privacy top of mind, I have explored a quirky but safe feeling brand. No aggressive tones, messaging, patterns, or visuals.

By combining the visual, motion and development process I was able to build the brand in an iterative fashion. Combining all three aspects while I am construction a visual helps create a balance that blends the three aspects into harmony.




Early Prototyping and Feedback

Getting feedback to assess the mental model of what people expect from a "visualization of their browser history" helps shape the product direction while in development.

Early stage prototypes like the one below have helped us:

  • Validate a specific experience when they sign up. By interacting with the prototype and then signing up, we know that people are not just interested in the general concept, but a specific interaction.
  • Gain real-life feedback into features and additional functionality.
